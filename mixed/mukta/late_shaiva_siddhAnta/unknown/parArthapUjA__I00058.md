Description = "RE31011 Parārthapūjā is a technical Śaiva manual written in Sanskrit and Tamil. It guides priests through Agamic temple rituals, traditional Hindu ceremonies performed for the welfare of the world.\n\nThe process begins with the seeking of permission from the site guardian (Kṣetrapāla), followed by the performance of extensive purification rituals for the environment, materials, and mantras. A key technical aspect is the preparation of Pañcagavya and Pañcāmṛta using precise geometric grids called Koṣṭhas. This action is preceded by worshipping the Sun and Gaṇeśa.\n\nThe core liturgy involves invoking Lord Śiva in the liṅga through a hierarchical ascent of five ritual seats (pañcāsana), using the prāsādamantra and specific mudras. The ceremony concludes with ritual bathing and the application of time-specific floral and cloth adornments, followed by final prayers for forgiveness to ensure the rite’s spiritual integrity.\n\nThe manuscript is in a damaged condition. It measures 3.3 cm x 35.5 cm."
