Description = "RE30578 Antyesthividhi. This palm-leaf manuscript bundle is a rare and well-preserved ceremonial text focusing on Viśeṣāntyeṣṭi. It specialises in final rites intended exclusively for those who have received Dīkṣā (initiation). It also addresses the second stage of initiation, known as Viśeṣadīkṣā, following the Putra-kannaku (the spiritual lineage/sonship) tradition.\n\nThe text is remarkable for its procedural clarity, providing a \"direct-facing\" (Ner-ethira) methodology for conducting the pūjā. It serves as a comprehensive guide to Adhvaśuddhi, meticulously mapping out the four Kalās: Śānti, Vidya, Pratiṣṭha, and Nivritti. For each Kalā, the manuscript provides a granular breakdown of its constituent Tattvas, Bhuvanas, Varṇas, Padas, and Mantras. Furthermore, it serves as a manual for the 12-day post-mortuary cycle, ensuring that every ritualistic requirement for the initiated soul is met with precision.\n\nThe codex is in excellent condition, showing no signs of decay or damage. Each folio is densely packed with information, containing between 6 and 11 lines of clearly inscribed script. The size is 3.8 cm x 18.5 cm."
