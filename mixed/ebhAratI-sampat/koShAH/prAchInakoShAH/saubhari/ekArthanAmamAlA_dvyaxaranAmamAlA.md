**INTRODUCTION**

 We are presenting here two kośas of Saubhari viz *Ekārthanāmamālā* and *Dvyakṣaranāmamālā*. The *Ekārthanāmamālā* deals with *Ekākṣaras* i. e. single consonants and the *Dvyakṣaranāmamālā *with double consonants. Though we have a number of *Ekākṣarakośas* written by different lexicographers, the one we have is different from them and is, therefore, unique in itself. In it the meanings are given not only to ka, kha; ga, gha, etc, but to *ka, kā, ki, kī*, etc.; *kha, khā, khi, khī* etc. also. Only in Buddhist *kośa* named *Abhidhānappadīpikā*, we find exactly a similar type of treatment given to vocables. The second work of Saubhari viz the *Dvyakṣaranāmamālā* is also quite peculiar in collecting together all the double consonants with meanings assigned to each of them. As far as the Editor of the present work knows, he has not come across such type of work in the whole range of *kośa* literature. Naturally a majority of the vocables from this *kośa* has not been recorded in any published Dictionaries.

 The present edition is based on the following MSS.

 1. The MS. named here as A, belongs to Anup Sanskrit Library Bikaner (No. 5453). It consists of 16 folios, each folio containing...12 lines on an average, It is written in legible Devanāgari characters. *Pṛsṭhamātrās* are invariably used. It always gives correct readings and is free from grammatical mistakes and scribal errors. It contains both the kośas and both of them are complete. It has marginal notes of the nature of a gloss. It is dated as Saṁvat 1639 and the colophon states that the MS. was written on Ravivāra, 9th day of the first half of Vaiśākha

 2. This MS, cited here as B belongs to the Government Collection (No. 20 and 21 of 1874–75) deposited at the Bhandarkar Oriental Research Institute, Poona. It is of the size of 8 3/4" x 4 1/8". It contains 21 leaves, each leaf having 7 to 9 lines on it on an average. It is written in Devanāgarī characters on a thick country paper. The handwriting is bold, legible and uniform. The borders of leaves are ruled in triple red lines. It contains both the works. It is not dated and its appearance is not very old. It also contains marginal notes of the nature of a gloss.

 3. This is another MS. called here as C belonging to the Government Collection (No. 581 of 1887–91) deposited at the Bhandarkar Oriental Research Institute, Poona. It contains 17 leaves, each leaf 9 1/4"x 5 5/8" in size and each leaf containing 11 lines to a page on an average. It is written on thick country paper in Devanāgarī characters. The handwriting is bold, clear, legible and uniform. Red pigment is used for verse numbers and yellow pigment is used for corrections. It contains both the works. The notes of the nature of a gloss are given in the margin. The MS, is not very old.

 4. This MS. named here as D also belongs to the Government collection (No. 577 of 1887–91) deposited at the Bhandarkar Oriental Research Institute, Poona. It has 6 folios, each folio 12 1/4" x 5 7/8" in size and each folio containing 11 lines on an average, It contains only *Ekārthanāmamālā*. It is written on a country paper in Devanāgarī characters, The meanings are given just above the vocables in between two lines for the first few passages, It being written most incorrectly, is not completely used for the present edition. But it gives sometimes good readings, not found in other three MSS, mentioned above.

 Some of the orthographical peculiarities of MSS. may be noted here.

\(1\) The avagrahas are often omitted in B, C and D.

\(2\) ch is often written for cch, e.g. *bhavechatruḥ \> bhavecchatruḥ, mlechaḥ \> mlecchaḥ.*

\(3\) v is used for b and vice verse, e. g. *vastaḥ\>bastaḥ, vṛhaspatiḥ\> bṛhaspatiḥ, vrahma\>brahma, vāsabaḥ\>vāsavaḥ, badana\>vadana, bivudhādhīśaḥ\>vibudhādhīśaḥ.*

\(4\) Whenever a consonant follows r the consonant is often doubled, e. g. *arkkaḥ\>arkaḥ, arcciḥ\>arciḥ, arjjunaḥ\>arjunaḥ, dharttā\>dhartā, aditir dditiḥ\>aditir ditiḥ, sarppaḥ\>sarpah.*

(5) *ṣ* is occassionally used for *kh*, e. g. *pāsaṇḍī\>Pākhaṇḍī, mūrṣaḥ\> mūrkhaḥ, śaṁṣaḥ\> śaṁkhaḥ, ṣaṭvā\>khaṭvā.*

\(6\) Very rarely *j* is used for *y, e. g. joniḥ\>yoniḥ.*

\(7\) *rg* is sometimes written *rgr*. e. g. *durgrā\>durgā, mārgraṇaḥ\> mārgaṇaḥ, gargraḥ\>gargaḥ.*

\(8\) *r* is used for r for a few times. e. g. *vraṣaḥ\>vṛṣaḥ, graham\>gṛha*

\(9\) In two cases the anusvāra is given unnecessarily, e. g. *braṁha\> brahma, kṣodaṁnam\>kṣodanam.*

(10) *ṭ* and *ṭh* are often undistinguishable, e.g. *pāpiṣṭaḥ\>pāpiṣṭhaḥ. śreṣṭā\>śreṣṭhā, suṣṭu\>suṣṭhu.*

\(11\) Many times we come across a phenomenon what is called 'phonetic movement' involving the presence or absence of a visarga, etc. e. g. *bhūmi\>bhūmiḥ, śyāvaḥ\>śāvaḥ.*

 (12) Sandhi rules are not strictly followed in a few cases. e. g. *śūdro śeḥ\> śūdraḥ śeḥ, putro ṣuḥ\>putraḥ ṣuḥ, kūrmo sūkarah\>kūrmaḥ sūkaraḥ, syāt bhoḥ\>syād bhoḥ.*

 All the peculiarities mentioned above are found more or less in all Mss, utilized for preparing the present edition. In preparing the edition all these peculiarities and other insignificant scribal mistakes have been silently corrected in the critical text. A particular care has been taken to give preference to the most suitable reading as evidenced by the Mss. material and the rejected readings have been relegated to the columns of foot–notes.

 The authorship of the present two works is ascribed to Saubhari. But no other information about him is available to us. He cannot be apparently identified with the Vedic sage of the same name who is believed to have compiled a few Vedic Sūktas. Neither we know anything about Angiras who is supposed to have known at least the *Ekārthanāmamālā *of Saubhari'. Under these circumstances it is very difficult to decide the date of the present works. We can only fix the lower limit of its date on the strength of one of the four Mss. utilized for the present edition, which is dated as Samvat 1639. So we conclude that the author of the present works must have flourished not later than Samvat 1639.

 एकार्थनाममाला—The title of the work is mentioned as मातृकानाममाला in the colophon of all the MSS, utilized for the edition.  13. परेतोऽण्डजः—ABC read परेतो जडः It is very difficult to accept one of the two. Both the meanings viz. जड and अण्डज are not found to have been assigned to ऊ in any lexicon.

 270. न्वं नवं वस्तु गद्यते—Should we construe नवं with वस्तु?

 274. म्वं खं ब्रह्म पुस्तकमुत्तमं—Similarly one is inclined to take उत्तमं as adjective qualifying पुस्तकं

 275. य्वा योपिज्जघनोत्तमा—Same as before. We should construe जघनोत्तमा with योषित्.

 279. श्वं प्रोक्तं शयने याने—The use of locatives in this line a rather unusual.

 283. पाप्मा गतिः सुखं—Apparently we should read पाप्मा with गतिः.

 286. पालनं तारणं पितुः—Are we to construe the line as पितुः पालनं and पितुः तारणं taking पितुः with both पालनं and तारणं?

 289 म्नं मनो मरणं मधु—C reads रमणं thus transposing the letters म and र.

 290. कलं कलनमुत्तमं—उत्तमं is to be construed with कलनं.

 296. गगनं शूलभृच्छिरः—Should we take शूलभृच्छिरः as one meaning?

  298. क्ष्लं क्षुद्रं वस्तु मरणं—Similarly क्षुद्रं वस्तु can be taken as one meaning.

 300. ख्ला सुरा क्षुद्रा योषित्कमललोचना—We have to take क्षुद्रा and कमललोचना as adjectives qualifying सुरा and याषिते respectively.

 301. ग्लं गलं गलनं सरत्—BC read सरित्, We read सरत् with A which generally gives correct readings. Moreover the author probably intends to give different shades of meaning in गलं गलनं and सरत् all of which can be assigned to the vocable ग्लं.

 306. वदनं सकलं वपुः—One is inclined to take सकलं वपुः as one meaning, especially when we read वदनं previously.

 307. ब्लं बलं ब्ला etc. Here the order of giving the vocables is curiously changed. Generally the author gives the vocable in order as it occurs in masculine, feminine and neuter. Should we read the 3rd and 4th pāda as the 1st and 2nd pada respectively to bring in the usual construction?

 312. ख्रो रवौ रासभे खले—This is another instance of author's giving the meanings in the locative case,

 322. च्रा चञ्चूश्च चमूश्चाटुः—AB read चटु by dropping the visarga. Is it an instance of case attraction, the form under consideration being influenced by which lies in its neighbourhood? or of phonetic change involving the dropping of visarga?

 323. च्रं मैथुनकमप्रजं—One is inclimed to construe अप्रजं as an adjective qualifying मैथुनकं Here in we get another instance of phonetic change. While reading मैथुनकमप्रजं the syllable a is emphasised, the phenomenon which tempts one to write visarga after म. Naturally C reads मैथुनकमः प्रजं.

 328. झ्रा जरा हानिरीश्वरी—Apparently ईश्वरी goes with हानिः.

 330. झ्रं जुष्टं भ्रमरैर्घुष्टं—we can construe भ्रमरैः both the ways, with जुष्टं as well as with घुष्टं.

 332. ट्रा स्मृतापूपा—can we read स्मृता and पूपा separately?

  339. ढ्रं फल्गु मरणं—Should we take फल्गु as an adjective qualifying मरणं? Similarly स्पन्दनाक्षमं can be construed with सुभ्व्रास्यं.

 342. ण्रं नाम ससुधं पात्रं वाक्यं नारदभाषितं—The line gives probably two meanings only.—ससुधं पात्रं and नारदभाषितं वाक्यं.

 344. तारणी सरमा रती—the lengthening ई in रती is metra causa.

 343, न्ना नारी नर्मदा दुष्टा नरवाङ्नरकोद्भवा—should we lake दुष्टा as qualifying नरवाङ्? Here again the order of vocables is changed.

 368. गङ्गातीरं पयः पेयं पाथः परं परं सरः—Should we construe पेयं with पयः? The meaning in the latter half is not clear.

 373. व्रो बृहस्पतिराचार्यो—Should we read आचार्यो as qualifying बृहस्पति? It is better to take आचार्यो as an independent meaning as बटुः is mentioned immediately after आचार्यो.

 392. व्रा श्रेष्ठा गिरिजा लक्ष्मी रत्नाकरगता तथा—रत्नाकरगता is to be construed with लक्ष्मी.

 405, हर्षयुक्ता च हस्तिनी—The epithet हर्षयुक्ता probably goes with हस्तिनी.
