Eleven years ago the Chalukya-Chola king Kulottunga I. was known only from the Chellur plates of his son Vira-Choda.[\[1\]](https://www.whatisindia.com/inscriptions/south_indian_inscriptions/volume_3/kulottunga_chola_i.html#_ftn1)
Since then a considerable number of other records has become accessible. Further versions of the pedigree of the last eastern Chalukya kings are contained in the Pithapuram plates of Vira-Choda[\[2\]](https://www.whatisindia.com/inscriptions/south_indian_inscriptions/volume_3/kulottunga_chola_i.html#_ftn2)
and in two of the Pithapuram pillar inscriptions.[\[3\]](https://www.whatisindia.com/inscriptions/south_indian_inscriptions/volume_3/kulottunga_chola_i.html#_ftn3)
In his valuable paper on the *Kalingattu-Parani[\[4\]](https://www.whatisindia.com/inscriptions/south_indian_inscriptions/volume_3/kulottunga_chola_i.html#_ftn4)*
Mr. V. Kanakasabhai Pillai gave an abridged translation of this Tamil poem and identified its hero with the Kulottunga I. of the Chellur plates. Dr. Fleetâ€™s paper on the chronology of the Eastern Chalukya kings contains an account of the reign of Kulottunga I.[\[5\]](https://www.whatisindia.com/inscriptions/south_indian_inscriptions/volume_3/kulottunga_chola_i.html#_ftn5)
based on all the materials which were available at the time.

The chief source for the history of Kulottunga I. are of course his own inscriptions. I subjoin a list of those which have been discovered so far, grouping them under eight heads for the sake of convenience.
