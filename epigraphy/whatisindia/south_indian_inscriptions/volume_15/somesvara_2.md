**No.2.**

**(B.K. No.207 of 1926-27)**

**NIDGUNDI, RON TALUK, DHARWAR DISTRICT**

On a stone set up in front of the Narayanadeva temple

**Bhuvanaikamalla (Somesvara II), 1074 A.D.**

This is dated Saka 996, Ananda, Asvija punnami, Tuesday, lunar eclipse, corresponding to 1074 A.D., October 7.

It records a gift of the village Kallamanapalli to the god Tripurusha by *Kannadagandhivigrahadhipati, Mahaprachanda-dandanayaka* Jannamayya, with the approval of the king. The donor is said to have had a temple constructed in honour of the deity at Nidungundi in Kisukadu Seventy.
The earlier portion contains a genealogical account of the Chalukya.
The epigraph is mutilated and worn out.
