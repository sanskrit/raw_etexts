# **THE CHALUKYAS OF KALYANA**

**No. 18 (Page No 15)**

**(B. K. No. 57 of 1935-36)**

**SAUNDATTI, PARASAGAD TALUK, BELGAUM DISTRICT**

**On a slab set up in the Basti-gudi Taila II A.D. 980**

The record is dated Saka 902, Vikrama, Paushya, su. 10, Thursday, Uttarayana-Sankranti. The Sankranti occurred on Thursday, December 23, A.D. 980. The tithi was, however, su. 14 on that day and not su. 10 as stated in the record.

The inscription registers the grant of 150 mattars of land by the Ratta chief Santivarmarasa to the Jaina monastery (basadi) caused to be constructed by him at **Sugandhavarti** (i.e., modern Saundatti). The genealogy of this chief is given. The gift was entrusted to Bahubali-bhattaraka. It also records another gift of 150 mattars of land by the chiefâ€™s mother Nijiyabharasi to the same monastery.

The record is published in JBBRAS. Vol. X, p. 204.

**No.19 (Page No 18)**

**(B. K. No.59 of 1937-38)**

**RUGI, INDI TALUK, BIJAPUR DISTRICT**

**Stone standing near the front wall of the Hanuman temple**

**(front and right sides) Ayyanadeva A.D. 1015**

This is dated Saka 936, Ananda, Phalguna, Amavasya, Sunday, Samkranti, corresponding to A.D.1015, February 20, Sunday, f.d.t. 56. The Mina samkranti occurred on the following day.

The record gives a genealogical account of the Chalukya dynasty in the beginning commencing from Ranaraga, son of Jayasimha I, and ending with vasudhaikamalla Ayyanadeva.

It registers a gift of 60 mattars of land, three gardens, one oil-mill and three shops, by Mahasamanta Devanarasa described as the brother of the king, to god Nagaresvara. Moradiya Chamundayya, the Senabova, wrote the record. It mentions chandrasekharacharya of Nagaresvara temple and also certain other individuals.

**No. 21 (Page No 21)**

**(B. K. No. 58 of 1937-38)**

**RUGI, INDI TALUK, BIJAPUR DISTRICT**

**Stone standing near the front wall of the Hanuman temple (back and left sides)**

**Jagadekamalla I (Jayasimha II) A.D. 1019**

The record is dated Saka 941, Siddharthin, Paushya, ba. 10 Thursday, Uttarayana-samkranti corresponding regularly to December 24, A.D. 1019.

It records that while the king was ruling from Etagiri, and his son Mahasamanta Dasarasa was governing the eighteen agraharas including Ruvige, situated in Tarddavadi-nadu, Mahasamanta Dhanasangrahayyarasa of the Selara family made a gift of land to God Nagaresvara of Aneya-Ruvige, for worship and offerings as also for the maintenance of the ascetics of the place. The gift was entrusted to Balachandrapandita.

The king is stated to have been ruling from his capital (nelevidu) Etagiri.

**No.22 (Page No 23)**

**(B. K. No. 26 of 1936-37)**

**GOLLAGERI, SINGI TALUK, BIJAPUR DISTRICT**

**Slab lying in front of the village Chavadi **

**Jagadekamalla I (Jayasimha II) A.D. 1027**

The record is dated Saka 9\[50\], Pra\[bhava\], vaisakha su, \[8\], Monday, corresponding to A.D. 1027, April 16, the weekday, however, being Sunday and not Monday. The Saka year was current.

It registers a grant of land to the temple built by Bachagaunda, by Mahasamanta Dandanatha Rebbarasa. The gift land was situated in his manya at Gollageri.
