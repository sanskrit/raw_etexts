......¹ Metre : Vasantatilaka.  
......² This *akshara* is rather an anomalous one,― between *sha* and *sa*. But I think that it is intended for *sha* : not for *sa*, by mistake for *sha*.― The word puzzled me. And I owe the reading of it to Dr. Hultzsch, who referred me, for an analogous expression, to *Ep. Ind.* Vol. I. p. 156, verse 13,― *Hara iva vṛisha-vihit-âsthô.*  
......³ Metre : Vaṁśastha.  
......⁴ Here we have a line in the Vasantatilaka metre,― evidently a quotation.― \[The same line forms part of verse 6 of an inscription of Kṛishṇa II. ; *Ep. Ind.* Vol. I.p. 54.― E.H.\]  
......⁵ The first two syllables of this word are perfectly clear and unmistakeable ; but the engraving of them is not quite complete,― owing apparently to the copper being particularly hard just here. There are other similar instances ; *e.g.* in the *b* of *śabda,* line 19, the *va* of *tripravara,* line 23, the second *va* of *nêvarêvaḥ,* line 30, and the syllables *rêva â,* line 30.  
......⁶ From here, to line 39, the construction is careless and faulty.
