*Venuvagāma (Sk. Veṇukagrāma),* dwelling-place of the nun Dhamarakhitā, the “native of Kosambī” (A 52), is stated [^(\[4\])](parta10.html#_f%0Atn4) to be a suburb of Kosambī and to have been identified by Cunningham with the modern village of Ben-Purwa to the north-east of Kosam. But the name seems more akin to *Beluvagāma* (also called *Beluvagāmaka* and *Belugāma,* a village near Vesāli *(Vaiśāli),* where the Buddha spent his last rainy season, according to the Mahāparinibbānasutta.
